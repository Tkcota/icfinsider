Every Major ICF Brand, Compared Independently

Nudura, Fox Blocks, Amvic, Element ICF, Quad-Lock, Super Form, Build Block — plus ICF deck systems. No brand pays to be ranked first. We compare them on specs, distribution, contractor feedback, and best use cases.

Independence notice: ICF Insider receives no compensation from any ICF manufacturer. Specs are sourced from manufacturer technical documents and publicly available data. Real-world performance varies by installation quality, climate, and project type.

How to Choose an ICF Brand

The honest truth: the brand matters less than the contractor. Every major ICF brand on this page produces a code-compliant, high-performing wall system. A skilled crew building with a mid-tier brand will outperform an inexperienced crew using a premium brand every time.

That said, brand selection does matter for a few real reasons:

  • Distributor availability — Some brands have strong regional distribution; others are thinly distributed in your area. If your contractor can't easily get the product, expect delays and higher costs.
  • Contractor familiarity — Most experienced ICF builders have a preferred brand they know well. Let your contractor's preference inform the decision.
  • Core width options — If you need a wider or narrower concrete core for structural or insulation reasons, some brands offer more flexibility than others.
  • Technical support — Larger brands (Nudura, Fox Blocks) have robust technical support teams. Smaller brands may have less resources for complex projects.
  • Commercial vs. residential — Some brands are better suited to commercial projects; others are optimized for residential.

Our recommendation: Ask your contractor which brand they've built the most with and feel most confident using. Then verify that brand is well-distributed in your area. Brand loyalty from an experienced contractor is a feature, not a red flag.

Wall System Brand Profiles

Here's what you need to know about each major ICF wall brand.

Nudura

Nudura is one of the most widely distributed ICF brands in North America, backed by Tremco (a division of RPM International). Acquired in 2015, Nudura benefits from the resources and distribution network of a large parent company, making it easier to source in more markets than many competitors.

The Nudura block uses an integrated plastic web tie system with a flat block profile. It's a reliable, well-tested product with strong technical support and a large installer network. Many large commercial and residential ICF projects across North America are built with Nudura.

Strengths

  • Widest distribution network in North America
  • Strong technical support and design resources
  • Large certified contractor network
  • Proven track record on large commercial projects
  • Multiple core width options including 12" for commercial

Considerations

  • Premium pricing vs. some competitors
  • Corporate parent means less flexibility on custom specs
  • Some contractors report block-to-block consistency issues in older production runs
Best for: Large residential builds, commercial projects, markets where Nudura has strong distributor presence. First choice when your contractor is Nudura-certified.
Fox Blocks
Parent company: Airlite Plastics Co.
US (Strong Midwest) American-Made Flat Block
R-23 Wall assembly R-value
6" Standard core width
16"×48" Standard block size

Fox Blocks is one of the most recognized ICF brands in the United States, manufactured by Airlite Plastics in Omaha, Nebraska. It's particularly dominant in the Midwest and has strong distribution throughout the US. Fox Blocks has invested heavily in marketing and education, making it one of the most builder-familiar brands in the market.

The Fox Block uses a flat block design with integrated plastic web ties. It's known for a consistent, clean block that stacks predictably and has good contractor documentation and training materials.

Strengths

  • American-manufactured — consistent quality control
  • Strong distributor network across the US
  • Excellent training and technical documentation
  • High brand recognition — easier to explain to homeowners
  • Large certified installer base, especially in the Midwest

Considerations

  • Less established in Canada than US-centric market suggests
  • Premium pricing in some markets
  • Fewer options for very high R-value assemblies vs. some competitors
Best for: US residential builds, especially in the Midwest. Excellent choice for first-time ICF homeowners who want a well-documented, widely recognized brand.

Amvic is a Canadian ICF brand with strong roots in cold-climate construction. It's a competitively priced flat-block system with solid performance specs and good distribution in both Canada and the northern United States.

Amvic is frequently chosen by value-conscious builders who want a proven product without the premium pricing of some larger brands. It's a solid performer with a straightforward block design and reliable technical specs.

Strengths

  • Competitive pricing vs. Nudura and Fox Blocks
  • Strong cold-climate performance track record
  • Good distribution in Canada and northern US
  • Simple, reliable flat-block design

Considerations

  • Smaller certified contractor network than top brands
  • Less brand recognition with homeowners
  • Fewer core width options than some competitors
  • Thinner technical support resources than Nudura or Fox Blocks
Best for: Value-conscious builds in Canada and the northern US. Good choice when your contractor prefers it and pricing matters.
Element ICF
Logix Brands Ltd. — formerly marketed as Logix ICF (retired Jan 1, 2025)
US & Canada Canadian Origin Hybrid Block + Panel
R-23+ Wall assembly R-value
6" Standard core width
16"×48" Standard block size
5 Core width options (4"–12")

Element ICF is the successor to Logix ICF, which was retired on January 1, 2025 after more than 20 years in the market. Both products are developed by Logix Brands Ltd. and are dimensionally compatible — projects designed with Logix ICF can be built with Element ICF without modification.

Element ICF is marketed as a hybrid ICF system — it combines preformed blocks and site-assembled panels that are fully interchangeable on the same project. This gives contractors flexibility: blocks for speed on straight runs, panels for custom dimensions and reduced shipping cost. The system ships to all US states and Canadian provinces through regional manufacturers including AMC Foam (central North America) and Beaver Plastics Ltd. (western North America).

Notable design features include 4-way reversible interlocks (corners work in both orientations), 24 zip tabs per block for form strength, full-height webs that prevent settling during the concrete pour, and built-in cut guides every 4 inches horizontally and 1 inch vertically to reduce cutting errors and waste.

Strengths

  • Unique hybrid system — blocks and panels interchangeable on same project
  • Full US and Canada availability as of 2024
  • 4-way reversible corners reduce waste and errors
  • Full-height webs prevent concrete settling during pour
  • Built-in cut guides speed up installation and reduce mistakes
  • Logix V12 line allows any 4-inch wall height increment without cutting
  • 20+ years of engineering data carried over from Logix ICF

Considerations

  • Brand is new (launched 2024) — contractor familiarity still building
  • Manufactured through regional partners, not company-owned plants
  • Smaller certified contractor network than Nudura or Fox Blocks currently
Best for: High-performance builds in Canada and cold-climate US markets. Strong choice for contractors who valued Logix ICF and are transitioning to Element, and for projects requiring flexible wall heights without custom cutting.

Super Form is a well-established Canadian ICF brand with a strong track record on both residential and commercial projects. It uses a conventional flat-block design with a good reputation for quality and consistency among experienced ICF builders.

Super Form is particularly well-regarded in western Canada and has a solid commercial project portfolio. It's a reliable mid-market choice that doesn't carry the premium pricing of top-tier brands.

Strengths

  • Strong reputation among experienced ICF contractors
  • Good commercial project track record
  • Reliable quality and consistency
  • Competitive pricing vs. premium brands

Considerations

  • Less distribution in the US than Canadian-focused brands
  • Lower brand recognition with US homeowners
  • Smaller technical support team than largest brands
Best for: Commercial projects and residential builds in western Canada. Good choice where contractor familiarity with the brand is strong.
Quad-Lock
Quad-Lock Building Systems
US & Canada Canadian Origin Panel + Separate Ties
R-24–40+ Wall assembly R-value

Quad-Lock stands apart from every other brand on this page because of its fundamentally different design. Rather than a single molded foam block with embedded ties, Quad-Lock uses separate flat EPS foam panels connected by individual polypropylene tie connectors.

This design allows for adjustable concrete core width — you can use the same foam panels with different tie lengths to achieve cores from 4" to 12" and beyond. It also means you can achieve higher R-values by using thicker foam panels on one or both faces. The tradeoff is that assembly is slightly more involved than standard interlocking blocks.

Strengths

  • Adjustable core width — most flexible concrete thickness on the market
  • Highest achievable R-values of any standard ICF system
  • Good for non-standard wall thicknesses and engineering requirements
  • Excellent for Passive House and ultra-high-performance builds
  • Flat panels ship and store more efficiently than molded blocks

Considerations

  • More assembly steps than standard interlocking blocks
  • Requires contractors familiar with the panel-and-tie system
  • Smaller installer base than Nudura or Fox Blocks
  • Less suitable for high-speed production builds
Best for: High-performance and Passive House builds where custom wall thickness and maximum R-value are priorities. Engineers and architects who need flexibility in concrete core specifications.
Build Block

Build Block is a US-manufactured ICF product positioned as an accessible, value-oriented option in the market. It uses a straightforward flat-block design that meets code requirements and performs well in standard residential applications.

Build Block is often chosen for its pricing and simplicity. It's a no-frills product that works well for builders who know ICF and don't need the additional features or technical support of premium brands.

Strengths

  • Competitive entry-level pricing
  • American-manufactured
  • Simple, familiar flat-block design
  • Meets all residential code requirements

Considerations

  • Smaller distribution network than top brands
  • Less technical support infrastructure
  • Fewer core width and specialty product options
  • Lower brand recognition — less useful for resale talking points
Best for: Budget-conscious residential builds with experienced ICF contractors who don't need manufacturer technical support.

ICF Deck & Floor Systems

ICF isn't just for walls. A growing category of products uses the same stay-in-place foam principle for floors, ceilings, and roofs — creating fully concrete structures with continuous insulation from foundation to roof deck.

Who uses deck systems? Primarily builders doing full concrete construction — multi-family, commercial, hurricane-zone residential, and high-performance builds where wood floor assemblies are undesirable for fire, sound, or resilience reasons.

Insul-Deck
Insul-Deck LLC
US & Canada Deck / Floor / Roof System
4-hr Fire rating
R-14+ Deck insulation value
Stays Foam removal needed
Floor+Roof Applications

Insul-Deck is the leading ICF deck system in North America. EPS foam deck forms are set in place between steel joists or directly on shoring, rebar is placed, and concrete is poured. The foam stays permanently, acting as the finished ceiling below and insulation within the floor assembly.

The result is a concrete floor or roof deck that's insulated, fire-rated to 4 hours, acoustically superior to wood framing, and extremely durable. It's the natural complement to ICF walls for builders going all-in on concrete construction.

Strengths

  • Creates a fully concrete, continuous building envelope when paired with ICF walls
  • 4-hour fire rating — exceptional life-safety performance
  • Superior sound dampening between floors
  • Permanent insulation — no separate insulation installation needed
  • Ideal for hurricane zones where wood floor assemblies are vulnerable

Considerations

  • Higher cost than wood floor framing
  • Requires experienced concrete deck crews
  • Heavier structural load — foundation and wall design must account for this
  • Longer pour and cure schedule than wood framing
Best for: Full concrete construction projects — multi-story ICF homes, commercial buildings, hurricane-zone construction, and high-performance builds where a complete concrete envelope is the goal.

Which Brand Is Right for Your Project?

Use this as a starting point — not a final answer. Your contractor's preference and local availability should carry the most weight.

Your Situation Brands to Prioritize
Standard US residential build Fox Blocks, Nudura, Build Block
Canadian residential build Nudura, Element ICF, Amvic, Super Form
Hurricane zone (FL, TX Gulf Coast) Fox Blocks, Nudura + Insul-Deck for floors
Cold climate / maximum energy performance Element ICF, Quad-Lock (highest R-values)
Passive House or ultra-high-performance Quad-Lock, Element ICF
Commercial or multi-family Nudura, Super Form, Fox Blocks + Insul-Deck
Budget-conscious residential Amvic, Build Block
Full concrete building envelope (walls + floors + roof) Any wall brand + Insul-Deck
