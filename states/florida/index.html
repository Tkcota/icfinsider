FAQ

Common Questions About ICF in Florida

Is ICF approved under the Florida Building Code?

Yes — and in most cases it exceeds it. Major ICF brands publish Florida product approvals including High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) compliance for South Florida. Ask your contractor for county-specific documentation.

Does an ICF home qualify for insurance discounts in Florida?

Usually yes. Florida insurers recognize concrete as significantly more storm-resistant and frequently offer premium discounts. Ask your insurer specifically about concrete construction discounts — savings can be substantial in coastal counties.

How much does an ICF home cost in Florida?

Expect a 5–12% upfront premium over comparable wood frame — roughly $160–$220+ per square foot above grade. Most Florida owners recover that gap within 8–12 years through lower energy bills and insurance savings.

Where is ICF most common in Florida?

Coastal areas with the highest hurricane risk — South Florida, Tampa Bay, the Gulf Coast, and the Space Coast — lead in ICF adoption. That said, ICF is used statewide for its energy and comfort benefits even away from the coast.
