FAQ

Common Questions About Insulated Concrete Form Construction in Hawaii

Is Insulated Concrete Form construction approved in Hawaii?

Yes. ICF systems are accepted under the Hawaii State Building Code. Given Hawaii's hurricane exposure, concrete construction is especially well-regarded by local building officials and insurers.

How does ICF handle Hawaii's tropical humidity and salt air?

Concrete is inherently resistant to moisture and salt corrosion — a major advantage over wood or metal framing in Hawaii's coastal environment. ICF homes require far less maintenance than wood-frame construction over time.

What does an ICF home cost in Hawaii?

Hawaii's construction costs are among the highest in the nation. ICF typically runs 8-15% more than comparable wood frame on the islands. Hawaii's very high electricity rates mean energy savings deliver faster payback — often 6-9 years.

Can ICF help with Hawaii's volcanic air quality issues?

ICF's airtight construction and excellent insulation can help maintain indoor air quality in areas affected by volcanic smog. Tight building envelopes reduce infiltration of outdoor particulates significantly.
