FAQ

Common Questions About ICF in Texas

Can an ICF home survive a Texas tornado?

ICF walls are significantly more tornado-resistant than wood frame. While no above-grade structure is guaranteed to survive a direct EF5 hit, ICF homes have survived EF3 and EF4 events that destroyed wood-framed structures nearby. Reinforced concrete provides the closest thing to a whole-house safe room.

How does ICF perform in Texas summers?

ICF's thermal mass and continuous insulation keep interior temperatures stable even during Texas's extreme heat. The concrete absorbs heat during the day and releases it slowly, dramatically reducing AC load. Most Texas ICF homeowners report 50–70% lower cooling bills.

How much does ICF construction cost in Texas?

ICF in Texas typically costs 5–15% more than comparable wood frame, with above-grade walls running $150–$215+ per square foot. The DFW, Houston, Austin, and San Antonio metros have the most competitive ICF contractor markets in the state.

Are there ICF contractors in Texas?

Yes — Texas has one of the largest ICF contractor bases in the country. Demand for storm-resilient construction drives a robust network of certified builders. All major ICF brands have certified contractors operating across the state.
