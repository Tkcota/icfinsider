Nudura ICF: Specs, Cost & Pros and Cons

Nudura is one of North America's most widely used ICF brands, known for its oversized block and deep distribution network. Here's an independent look at the specs, cost, and honest pros and cons, plus how to find a Nudura pro near you.

About the Brand

What Is Nudura ICF?

Nudura

Parent company: Tremco (RPM International)  |  Canadian origin  |  Flat block system

Nudura is a Canadian-origin ICF brand now owned by Tremco, a division of RPM International, one of the largest building products companies in the world. That corporate backing gives Nudura a distribution network few ICF brands can match, making it one of the most available products across North America.

Nudura uses a flat block design with an integrated plastic web tie system. The block itself is larger than most competitors: 18 inches tall by 8 feet long, meaning more square footage per block and faster installation. Nudura's patented DuraFold hinge system lets web ties fold flat during shipping, fitting up to 40% more product per truckload, which reduces freight costs and cuts the number of deliveries needed on the job site.

R-22 to R-28 Wall assembly R-value
6" Most common core size
18" x 96" Standard block size
4" to 12" Core width options

Core widths in the Nudura system range from 4" through 12", giving engineers flexibility on structural requirements, from standard residential walls up to the wider cores used in high-wind zones and large commercial projects.

The Honest Take

Nudura Pros and Cons

No ICF brand is perfect for every project. Here's a straight, unbiased look at where Nudura leads and where it trails the competition.

Strengths

  • Widest distribution network in North America
  • Strong technical support and design resources
  • Large certified contractor network
  • Proven track record on large commercial projects
  • Multiple core width options, including 12" for commercial
  • 18" tall x 8' long block, more sq ft per block than most competitors
  • DuraFold hinged web ships flat, up to 40% more product per truck and lower freight costs

Considerations

  • Premium pricing versus some competitors
  • Corporate parent can mean less flexibility on custom specs
  • Some contractors report block-to-block consistency issues in older production runs

Best for: large residential builds, commercial and multi-family projects, hurricane and high-wind zones, and any project where wide distribution and a deep certified-contractor network matter, especially when your contractor is already Nudura-certified.

Cost Expectations

What Does Nudura ICF Cost?

Nudura carries a slight price premium compared to some ICF competitors, but it's generally competitive with Fox Blocks and other major brands.

What the forms cost: A standard Nudura form covers 12 sq ft (18" tall by 8' long). Before freight, the forms typically run $55 to $72 per form, or about $4.58 to $6.00 per sq ft, depending on your location. Freight cost not included. If you're buying from a local warehouse or distributor and the price runs higher than this range, either freight is already baked in (common for local pickup), or you're paying a premium and it's worth pricing it elsewhere.

Updated June 22, 2026. We monitor price increases across the ICF industry.

The forms are only one piece of the total, though. These factors drive the rest:

  • Design complexity: simple rectangular footprints install fastest; multi-story or irregular designs add labor time
  • Core width: wider cores (8", 10", 12") use more concrete and cost more than a standard 6" residential wall
  • Your local market: labor rates, contractor availability, and demand vary widely by region
  • Concrete pricing: concrete is a major ICF cost driver and fluctuates with local market conditions
  • Engineering requirements: high-wind, seismic, or coastal zones may require additional structural engineering

Where to Buy

Where to Buy Nudura

Nudura is sold through regional distributors, and they're usually the best first call on any ICF project. A good distributor knows the product inside and out, can give you real pricing for your area, and is connected with the local builders and contractors who install it. Their job is to support your project and supply the product, so they have every reason to help you get moving.

A money-saving tip: where you can, buy the blocks directly from the distributor rather than through your builder. Builders typically add a markup on materials they supply, which is normal, but sourcing the forms yourself and having your builder install them can save that margin. Even if your builder prefers to supply the material, knowing the distributor's direct price gives you real negotiating leverage.

FAQ

Common Questions About Nudura ICF

What makes Nudura different from other ICF brands?

Nudura uses a larger block than most competitors (18" tall by 8' long) and a patented DuraFold hinged web that ships flat, fitting up to 40% more product per truckload. Backed by Tremco (RPM International), it also has one of the widest distribution networks in North America.

What is the R-value of Nudura ICF?

A standard Nudura wall assembly delivers roughly R-22 to R-28 depending on configuration, combining continuous EPS foam insulation with the thermal mass of the concrete core. Core widths range from 4" to 12".

How much does Nudura ICF cost?

Nudura carries a slight premium over some competitors but is generally competitive with other major brands. Total project cost depends far more on design complexity, core width, concrete and labor pricing in your area, and engineering requirements than on the brand of block itself.

Is Nudura better than Fox Blocks?

Both are excellent flat-block ICF systems with similar R-values and core options. Nudura has a larger block and wider distribution through Tremco; Fox Blocks is American-made with strong Midwest distribution. The better choice usually comes down to which system your contractor is certified and experienced with.
