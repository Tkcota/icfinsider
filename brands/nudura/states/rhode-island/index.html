About the Brand

What Is Nudura ICF?

Nudura

Parent company: Tremco (RPM International)  |  Canadian origin  |  Flat block system

Nudura is a Canadian-origin ICF brand now owned by Tremco, a division of RPM International, one of the largest building products companies in the world. That corporate backing gives Nudura a distribution network that few ICF brands can match, making it one of the most available products across North America, including Rhode Island.

Nudura uses a flat block design with an integrated plastic web tie system. The block itself is larger than most competitors: 18 inches tall by 8 feet long, meaning more square footage per block and faster installation. Nudura's patented DuraFold hinge system allows web ties to fold flat during shipping, which reduces freight costs and cuts the number of deliveries needed on the job site.

Core widths range from 4" through 12". In Rhode Island's coastal and Narragansett Bay regions, engineers frequently specify wider cores to meet high-wind zone load requirements. Inland residential projects typically use the standard 6" core.

Why It Makes Sense

Why Rhode Island Builders Choose Nudura

Rhode Island's exposure to Narragansett Bay and the Atlantic brings real hurricane and nor'easter risk, while cold New England winters create significant heating demands. Nudura ICF handles both effectively.

Coastal and Hurricane Storm Protection

Rhode Island's coastline and Narragansett Bay communities face regular hurricane, tropical storm, and nor'easter exposure. ICF walls, including Nudura, provide meaningful structural protection in high-wind and storm events compared to wood framing. Coastal Rhode Island contractors increasingly specify ICF for new construction in storm-exposed areas.

Cold New England Winter Performance

Rhode Island winters are cold, with heating season running from October through April. Nudura's R-22 to R-28 wall assembly provides continuous insulation with no thermal bridging, reducing heating loads significantly compared to wood frame construction.

Rhode Island Energy Code Compliance

Rhode Island follows a demanding residential energy code aligned with the Northeast's strict standards. Nudura's R-22 to R-28 wall assembly helps meet and often exceed Rhode Island's thermal performance requirements, simplifying compliance for your design team.

Rhode Island and Regional Distribution

Nudura's distribution network covers Rhode Island through its regional New England supply chain, with contractors in Providence, Warwick, Newport, and surrounding markets able to source product reliably. Your contractor should confirm current availability and lead times.

Cost Expectations

What Does Nudura ICF Cost in Rhode Island?

Nudura carries a slight price premium compared to some ICF competitors, but it's generally competitive with other major brands. In Rhode Island, total ICF project costs depend on several factors beyond the block itself:

  • Design complexity: Simple rectangular footprints are fastest; complex multi-story or irregular designs add labor time
  • Core width: Wider cores (8", 10", 12") increase both block and concrete costs and are required in some structural and wind zone applications
  • Location in Rhode Island: Rhode Island has relatively high construction labor costs as part of the New England market. Newport and coastal communities see higher demand than inland areas. Coastal projects with wind zone requirements may need additional engineering for code compliance.
  • Concrete pricing: Concrete is a major cost driver in ICF construction and fluctuates with local market conditions
  • Engineering requirements: Local wind zones, seismic categories, and code requirements may call for additional structural engineering

The best way to understand what Nudura ICF will cost for your specific project is to connect with a Rhode Island contractor who has direct experience with the system.
