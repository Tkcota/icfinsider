About the Brand

What Is Nudura ICF?

Nudura

Parent company: Tremco (RPM International)  |  Canadian origin  |  Flat block system

Nudura is a Canadian-origin ICF brand now owned by Tremco, a division of RPM International, one of the largest building products companies in the world. That corporate backing gives Nudura a distribution network that few ICF brands can match, making it one of the most available products across North America, including Michigan.

Nudura uses a flat block design with an integrated plastic web tie system. The block itself is larger than most competitors: 18 inches tall by 8 feet long, meaning more square footage per block and faster installation. Nudura's patented DuraFold hinge system allows web ties to fold flat during shipping, which reduces freight costs and cuts the number of deliveries needed on the job site.

Core widths range from 4" through 12". In Michigan's cold climate zones, wider cores are often specified to maximize thermal performance. Nudura's 5 core width options give structural engineers and designers flexibility to meet Michigan's energy code requirements.

Why It Makes Sense

Why Michigan Builders Choose Nudura

Michigan's winters are among the harshest in the contiguous US, with lake effect snow adding moisture and cold exposure across much of the state. Nudura ICF handles Michigan's climate demands well for several reasons.

Extreme Cold Weather Performance

Michigan winters are long and cold, with lake effect snow amplifying the challenge in the western and northern parts of the state. Nudura's R-22 to R-28 wall assembly provides continuous insulation that significantly reduces heating loads compared to wood frame construction.

Lake Effect Snow and Moisture Resistance

Western Michigan in particular sees extreme lake effect snow and year-round moisture exposure. ICF walls resist moisture infiltration and don't rot or degrade the way wood framing can under sustained wet conditions. Nudura's system handles Great Lakes climate demands well over the long term.

Energy Savings in a Heating-Dominant Climate

Michigan homeowners spend significantly on heating. Nudura's high R-value wall system reduces that burden, and ICF's continuous insulation with no thermal bridging outperforms insulated wood stud walls in cold conditions. The payback on ICF's premium comes faster in heating-dominant climates.

Michigan Market Distribution

Nudura's distribution network covers Michigan, with supply available to contractors in the Detroit, Grand Rapids, Lansing, and Traverse City markets. Your contractor should confirm distributor availability and lead times for your specific area.

Cost Expectations

What Does Nudura ICF Cost in Michigan?

Nudura carries a slight price premium compared to some ICF competitors, but it's generally competitive with other major brands. In Michigan, total ICF project costs depend on several factors beyond the block itself:

  • Design complexity: Simple rectangular footprints are fastest; complex multi-story or irregular designs add labor time
  • Core width: Wider cores (8", 10", 12") increase both block and concrete costs and are required in some structural and wind zone applications
  • Location in Michigan: Michigan construction costs vary by region, with the Detroit and Grand Rapids metros having more experienced ICF labor available. Northern Michigan and rural areas may see higher project costs due to crew travel and logistics.
  • Concrete pricing: Concrete is a major cost driver in ICF construction and fluctuates with local market conditions
  • Engineering requirements: Local wind zones, seismic categories, and code requirements may call for additional structural engineering

The best way to understand what Nudura ICF will cost for your specific project is to connect with a Michigan contractor who has direct experience with the system.
