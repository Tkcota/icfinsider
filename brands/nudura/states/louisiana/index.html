About the Brand

What Is Nudura ICF?

Nudura

Parent company: Tremco (RPM International)  |  Canadian origin  |  Flat block system

Nudura is a Canadian-origin ICF brand now owned by Tremco, a division of RPM International, one of the largest building products companies in the world. That corporate backing gives Nudura a distribution network that few ICF brands can match, making it one of the most available products across North America, including Louisiana.

Nudura uses a flat block design with an integrated plastic web tie system. The block itself is larger than most competitors: 18 inches tall by 8 feet long, meaning more square footage per block and faster installation. Nudura's patented DuraFold hinge system allows web ties to fold flat during shipping, which reduces freight costs and cuts the number of deliveries needed on the job site.

Core widths range from 4" through 12". In Louisiana's coastal and hurricane-exposed regions, engineers frequently specify wider cores with heavier rebar schedules to meet high-wind load requirements. Inland projects commonly use the standard 6" core.

Why It Makes Sense

Why Louisiana Builders Choose Nudura

Louisiana faces some of the most demanding building conditions in the country: direct hurricane exposure, extreme humidity, and summer heat that stretches most of the year. Nudura ICF is well-suited to handle all of it.

Hurricane Zone Performance

Louisiana sits squarely in the Gulf Coast hurricane zone. ICF walls, including Nudura, have a strong track record in high-wind events. The reinforced concrete core resists the lateral forces and debris impact that cause most storm damage, and many Louisiana homeowners rebuild in ICF after experiencing hurricane damage firsthand.

Extreme Humidity and Moisture Resistance

Louisiana's climate is among the most humid in the country. Wood-frame homes face constant moisture pressure, rot risk, and mold exposure. ICF walls don't absorb moisture, don't rot, and don't support mold growth. Nudura's concrete and EPS foam system holds up in Louisiana's climate over the long term.

Energy Performance in Extreme Heat

Louisiana summers are long, hot, and oppressive. Nudura's R-22 to R-28 wall assembly significantly reduces cooling loads, and ICF's thermal mass stabilizes interior temperatures. Many Louisiana ICF homeowners see meaningful reductions in monthly AC costs compared to wood-frame construction.

Louisiana Distribution

Nudura's national distribution network covers Louisiana, with supply available to contractors in the New Orleans, Baton Rouge, Shreveport, and Lafayette markets. Your contractor should confirm current distributor availability and lead times for your specific project.

Cost Expectations

What Does Nudura ICF Cost in Louisiana?

Nudura carries a slight price premium compared to some ICF competitors, but it's generally competitive with other major brands. In Louisiana, total ICF project costs depend on several factors beyond the block itself:

  • Design complexity: Simple rectangular footprints are fastest; complex multi-story or irregular designs add labor time
  • Core width: Wider cores (8", 10", 12") increase both block and concrete costs and are required in some structural and wind zone applications
  • Location in Louisiana: Labor rates and ICF contractor availability are strongest in the Baton Rouge and New Orleans metros. Coastal and rural Louisiana projects may see higher costs due to crew travel, logistics, and the additional engineering required for high-wind zones.
  • Concrete pricing: Concrete is a major cost driver in ICF construction and fluctuates with local market conditions
  • Engineering requirements: Local wind zones, seismic categories, and code requirements may call for additional structural engineering

The best way to understand what Nudura ICF will cost for your specific project is to connect with a Louisiana contractor who has direct experience with the system.
