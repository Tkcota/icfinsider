Nudura ICF in Florida: Build Guide & Contractors

Nudura is one of the most widely used ICF brands in Florida's hurricane zone, favored for its large block size, strong distribution network, and proven performance in high-wind conditions. If you're building with Insulated Concrete Forms in Florida and your contractor works with Nudura, here's what you need to know.

About the Brand

What Is Nudura ICF?

Nudura

Parent company: Tremco (RPM International)  |  Canadian origin  |  Flat block system

Nudura is a Canadian-origin ICF brand now owned by Tremco, a division of RPM International, one of the largest building products companies in the world. That corporate backing gives Nudura a distribution network that few ICF brands can match, making it one of the most available products across North America, including Florida.

Nudura uses a flat block design with an integrated plastic web tie system. The block itself is larger than most competitors: 18 inches tall by 8 feet long, meaning more square footage per block and faster installation. Nudura's patented DuraFold hinge system allows web ties to fold flat during shipping, which reduces freight costs and cuts the number of deliveries needed on the job site.

Core widths in the Nudura system range from 4" through 12", giving engineers flexibility on structural requirements. That range is especially important for Florida's wind exposure categories and hurricane load calculations.

Why It Makes Sense

Why Florida Builders Choose Nudura

Florida's combination of hurricane risk, heat, humidity, and strict building codes makes ICF one of the most practical wall systems for new construction. Nudura in particular is well-positioned for the Florida market for several reasons.

Hurricane Zone Performance

ICF walls (including Nudura) have a strong track record in high-wind events. The combination of reinforced concrete and continuous insulation creates a monolithic wall that outperforms wood framing in storm conditions. Many Florida contractors and engineers specify Nudura by name in hurricane-zone projects.

Florida Building Code Ready

Nudura meets Florida Building Code requirements across the state's multiple wind zones. Your structural engineer will specify the appropriate core width and rebar schedule for your location. Nudura's 5 core width options (4" through 12") give engineers the flexibility to meet any load requirement.

Energy Performance in the Heat

Florida homeowners run AC for most of the year. Nudura's R-22 to R-28 wall assembly, combined with ICF's thermal mass, significantly reduces cooling loads compared to wood frame construction. Many Florida ICF homeowners report meaningful reductions in monthly energy bills.

Strong Florida Distribution

Backed by Tremco's national network, Nudura has reliable distributor coverage throughout Florida, from Tampa and Orlando to South Florida and the Gulf Coast. Product availability matters on a job site, and Nudura's distribution strength means fewer sourcing headaches for your contractor.

Cost Expectations

What Does Nudura ICF Cost in Florida?

Nudura carries a slight price premium compared to some ICF competitors, but it's generally competitive with Fox Blocks and other major brands. In Florida, total ICF project costs depend on several factors beyond the block itself:

  • Design complexity: Simple rectangular footprints are fastest; complex multi-story or irregular designs add labor time
  • Core width: Wider cores (8", 10", 12") are required in some hurricane zones, which affects both block and concrete costs
  • Location in Florida: Labor rates and contractor availability vary between South Florida, the Gulf Coast, Central Florida, and the Panhandle
  • Concrete pricing: Concrete is a major cost driver in ICF construction and fluctuates with local market conditions
  • Engineering requirements: Florida's wind exposure categories and coastal building zones may require additional structural engineering

The best way to understand what Nudura ICF will cost for your specific project is to connect with a Florida contractor who has direct experience with the system.
