About the Brand

What Is Nudura ICF?

Nudura

Parent company: Tremco (RPM International)  |  Canadian origin  |  Flat block system

Nudura is a Canadian-origin ICF brand now owned by Tremco, a division of RPM International, one of the largest building products companies in the world. That corporate backing gives Nudura a distribution network that few ICF brands can match, making it one of the most available products across North America, including Oregon.

Nudura uses a flat block design with an integrated plastic web tie system. The block itself is larger than most competitors: 18 inches tall by 8 feet long, meaning more square footage per block and faster installation. Nudura's patented DuraFold hinge system allows web ties to fold flat during shipping, which reduces freight costs and cuts the number of deliveries needed on the job site.

Why It Makes Sense

Why Oregon Builders Choose Nudura

Oregon's building environment is shaped by seismic risk, year-round Pacific moisture, and one of the more stringent energy codes in the country. Nudura ICF addresses all three effectively.

Seismic Performance

Oregon sits on the Cascadia Subduction Zone, one of the most seismically active fault systems on the continent. ICF's reinforced concrete wall system handles seismic lateral loads better than wood framing. Nudura's range of core widths (4" through 12") gives engineers flexibility to meet Oregon's seismic design requirements for your specific location.

Pacific Northwest Moisture and Rain Resistance

Western Oregon receives significant annual rainfall and sustained moisture exposure. ICF walls resist moisture infiltration and don't rot or support mold growth the way wood framing can under prolonged wet conditions. This is a meaningful durability advantage in Oregon's climate over the long term.

Oregon Energy Code Compliance

Oregon has a demanding residential energy code. Nudura's R-22 to R-28 wall assembly helps meet and exceed Oregon's thermal envelope requirements, and ICF's continuous insulation with no thermal bridging performs well under the state's energy compliance pathways.

Oregon Distribution

Nudura's distribution network covers Oregon, with supply available to contractors in Portland, Eugene, Salem, and Bend markets. Your contractor should confirm current distributor availability and lead times for your specific project location.

Cost Expectations

What Does Nudura ICF Cost in Oregon?

Nudura carries a slight price premium compared to some ICF competitors, but it's generally competitive with other major brands. In Oregon, total ICF project costs depend on several factors beyond the block itself:

  • Design complexity: Simple rectangular footprints are fastest; complex multi-story or irregular designs add labor time
  • Core width: Wider cores (8", 10", 12") increase both block and concrete costs and are required in some structural and wind zone applications
  • Location in Oregon: Portland has a strong construction market with competitive ICF labor. Eugene, Salem, and Bend are smaller but growing markets. Coastal Oregon projects may see additional engineering requirements for wind and seismic exposure, affecting total cost.
  • Concrete pricing: Concrete is a major cost driver in ICF construction and fluctuates with local market conditions
  • Engineering requirements: Local wind zones, seismic categories, and code requirements may call for additional structural engineering

The best way to understand what Nudura ICF will cost for your specific project is to connect with an Oregon contractor who has direct experience with the system.
