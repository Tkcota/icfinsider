About the Brand

What Is Nudura ICF?

Nudura

Parent company: Tremco (RPM International)  |  Canadian origin  |  Flat block system

Nudura is a Canadian-origin ICF brand now owned by Tremco, a division of RPM International, one of the largest building products companies in the world. That corporate backing gives Nudura a distribution network that few ICF brands can match, making it one of the most available products across North America, including Wyoming.

Nudura uses a flat block design with an integrated plastic web tie system. The block itself is larger than most competitors: 18 inches tall by 8 feet long, meaning more square footage per block and faster installation. Nudura's patented DuraFold hinge system allows web ties to fold flat during shipping, which reduces freight costs and cuts the number of deliveries needed on the job site.

Why It Makes Sense

Why Wyoming Builders Choose Nudura

Wyoming is one of the most demanding building environments in the country: extreme cold, persistent high winds, high altitude, and vast rural geography. Nudura ICF handles each of these challenges better than standard wood framing.

Extreme Cold and Wind Performance

Wyoming experiences extreme cold in winter, compounded by some of the highest sustained wind speeds in the continental US. Nudura's R-22 to R-28 wall assembly provides continuous insulation, and ICF's monolithic concrete core resists wind-driven air infiltration that standard wood-frame walls cannot prevent. Together, these make a dramatic difference in Wyoming's conditions.

Energy Savings in a Heating-Dominant Climate

Wyoming homeowners face long, expensive heating seasons. Nudura's high R-value wall system significantly reduces heating demand, and in a state where heating costs are a major annual expense, ICF's energy performance translates directly to meaningful annual savings.

Structural Durability in Mountain Conditions

Wyoming's freeze-thaw cycles, snowpack, and high-altitude conditions put real stress on building materials. ICF walls handle freeze-thaw cycles better than wood framing, resist moisture infiltration, and maintain structural integrity through Wyoming's demanding seasonal and environmental conditions over the long term.

Wyoming Distribution

Nudura's distribution network reaches Wyoming, with supply available to contractors in Cheyenne, Casper, Gillette, and Jackson markets. Wyoming's vast geography means freight logistics and lead times vary significantly by location. Your contractor should confirm specifics for your project area well in advance.

Cost Expectations

What Does Nudura ICF Cost in Wyoming?

Nudura carries a slight price premium compared to some ICF competitors, but it's generally competitive with other major brands. In Wyoming, total ICF project costs depend on several factors beyond the block itself:

  • Design complexity: Simple rectangular footprints are fastest; complex multi-story or irregular designs add labor time
  • Core width: Wider cores (8", 10", 12") increase both block and concrete costs and are required in some structural and wind zone applications
  • Location in Wyoming: Wyoming's construction market is limited by sparse population and vast geography. Cheyenne and Casper have the most active contractor markets. Jackson and resort communities have high labor costs. Remote and rural Wyoming projects require significant advance planning for materials, crew, and logistics.
  • Concrete pricing: Concrete is a major cost driver in ICF construction and fluctuates with local market conditions
  • Engineering requirements: Local wind zones, seismic categories, and code requirements may call for additional structural engineering

The best way to understand what Nudura ICF will cost for your specific project is to connect with a Wyoming contractor who has direct experience with the system.
